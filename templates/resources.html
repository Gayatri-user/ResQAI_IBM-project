Resource Management Quick Reference

Water & Food Standards
  • Water: Minimum 15 litres per person per day (drinking, cooking, sanitation).
  • Drinking water: 3–5 litres per person per day minimum.
  • Food: 2,100 kcal per adult per day; prioritise non-perishable, easy-to-prepare foods.
  • Distribution: Set up dedicated distribution points; log all distributions.
  • Special diets: Identify diabetics, infants, pregnant women, allergy sufferers.
  • Sanitation: 1 toilet per 20 persons minimum; female-only facilities required.
Emergency Shelter Standards
  • Space: 3.5 m² per person minimum (covered sleeping area).
  • Registration: Register all displaced persons on arrival for tracking.
  • Security: Designate safe areas for women, children, and vulnerable persons.
  • Medical: Establish first aid/triage station; coordinate EMS access.
  • Communication: Post emergency numbers, shelter rules, and daily briefings.
  • Pets: Designate a separate area — many evacuees will not leave without pets.
Volunteer Coordination
  • Register and credential all volunteers before deployment.
  • Assign team leaders for each functional area (food, shelter, logistics).
  • Use shift schedules — prevent volunteer burnout over multi-day operations.
  • Provide safety briefings for all volunteers before they enter the field.
  • Establish a volunteer coordination hub separate from the main shelter.
  • Coordinate with established organisations (Red Cross, FEMA, CERT teams).
Supply Chain & Logistics
  • Identify and pre-position supplies before a predicted disaster where possible.
  • Establish a central warehouse with dedicated receiving and distribution teams.
  • Track all incoming and outgoing supplies with a manifest log.
  • Prioritise fuel, generators, and communications equipment for sustained ops.
  • Coordinate with local businesses for emergency procurement agreements.
  • Establish air and ground transport corridors with emergency management EOC.
