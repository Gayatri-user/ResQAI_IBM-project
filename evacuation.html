Evacuation Quick Reference

Universal Evacuation Principles
  1. Follow official evacuation orders — do NOT delay or shelter in place when ordered to evacuate.
  2. Activate community alert systems (sirens, cell broadcasts, social media).
  3. Establish clear assembly points at least 500m from the hazard zone.
  4. Use designated evacuation routes — avoid improvised shortcuts.
  5. Account for all persons — use roll calls at assembly points.
  6. Prioritise vulnerable populations: elderly, disabled, children, pets.
  7. Assign zone-based evacuation sequencing to prevent congestion.
  8. Maintain communication with regional emergency operations center (EOC).
72-Hour Go Bag Essentials
  1. Water: 1 gallon per person per day (3-day supply minimum).
  2. Non-perishable food and manual can opener.
  3. Copies of IDs, insurance documents, and emergency contacts.
  4. Cash in small bills (ATMs may be offline).
  5. Flashlight, spare batteries, and portable phone charger.
  6. Battery-powered or hand-crank emergency weather radio.
  7. Prescription medications (at least 7-day supply).
  8. Warm clothing, rain gear, and sturdy closed-toe shoes.
Earthquake Immediate Evacuation
  1. DROP, COVER, HOLD ON until shaking stops.
  2. Once shaking stops, evacuate immediately — do not use elevators.
  3. Watch for falling debris, broken glass, and downed power lines.
  4. Move to pre-designated open assembly areas away from structures.
  5. Do NOT re-enter buildings until cleared by structural engineers.
  6. Be prepared for aftershocks — they can trigger secondary evacuations.
Wildfire Evacuation Protocol
  1. Evacuate immediately when ordered — do NOT wait to see the fire.
  2. Close all windows and doors to slow fire spread before leaving.
  3. Take designated evacuation routes; avoid roads parallel to fire spread.
  4. Wear N95 mask or wet cloth to protect from smoke inhalation.
  5. If trapped by fire: pull off road, turn on headlights, stay in vehicle.
  6. Report location to emergency services and activate SOS signal.
